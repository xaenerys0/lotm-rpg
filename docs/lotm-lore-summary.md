## Core Metaphysics: The Three Laws of Beyonder Characteristics

Beyonder characteristics are indestructible, finite physical entities that form
the "source code" of all supernatural power. They are not a renewable resource.

### 1. Law of Indestructibility

The total volume of characteristics in the cosmos is constant. They cannot be
created or destroyed, only transferred from one carrier to another. When a
Beyonder or mystical creature dies, their characteristics condense into a
single recoverable item — a physical organ, crystal, or supernatural artifact.

### 2. Law of Conservation (Similar-Sequence)

A zero-sum hierarchy governs each of the 22 Standard Pathways. Advancement
consumes characteristics of the current sequence to produce the next. At the
apex, advancing to Sequence 0 (True God) requires absorbing all three available
Sequence 1 characteristics for that pathway. While a god occupies the throne,
no Sequence 1 Angels of that pathway can exist.

### 3. Law of Convergence (Aggregation)

Characteristics of the same or neighboring pathways are unconsciously drawn
toward each other — a metaphysical gravitational pull. In narrative terms this
manifests as "fate": Beyonders frequently encounter enemies, artifacts, and
ingredients relevant to their next advancement. This law drives encounters and
explains why powerful Beyonders inevitably clash.

## The Potion System and the Acting Method

### Potions

The potion system was popularized by the First and Second Blasphemy Slates.
Each of the 22 pathways divides into ten sequences (Sequence 9 through
Sequence 0). Beyonders advance by brewing and drinking potions that integrate
characteristics into their body. Drinking a potion without proper mental
preparation causes **loss of control** — the Beyonder's body and mind collapse
under the Original Creator's residual mental imprints, transforming them into a
mindless monster.

### The Acting Method

The primary mechanism for safely digesting a potion. The Beyonder must behave
according to the symbolic meaning of their sequence name:

- A **Seer** performs divinations and speaks with mystery
- A **Clown** maintains a humorous facade and performs for audiences
- A **Spectator** observes people without interfering

The practitioner must always **"remember that they are only acting."** Total
immersion in the role leads to loss of self-identity and eventual insanity.
Successful acting allows the individual's consciousness to fuse with the
characteristic's power while deceiving its mental imprint.

## Sequence Classifications

| Rank    | Classification   | Attributes and Risks                                                     |
|---------|------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Seq 9–8 | Low-Sequence     | Marginal supernatural abilities; generally vulnerable to conventional firearms |
| Seq 7–5 | Mid-Sequence     | Versatile abilities; advancement requires rituals; common period for initial onset of madness |
| Seq 4–3 | Saint (Demigod)  | Attainment of "godhood"; incomplete Mythical Creature Form; 500-year lifespan |
| Seq 2–1 | Angel (Demigod)  | Complete Mythical Creature Form; can respond to prayers; require "Anchors" to maintain sanity |
| Seq 0   | True God         | Singular existence; absolute authority over pathway; capable of rewriting reality |

**Anchors** (Seq 2+): High-sequence Beyonders risk losing themselves to the
accumulated mental imprints of characteristics. Anchors are emotional
connections to the human world — people, places, identities — that keep the
Beyonder grounded in their own consciousness.

## Historical Chronology: The Five Epochs

### First Epoch — Age of Chaos

The "Pre-Epoch" refers to modern Earth, ending ~2020 when the Original Creator
awakened, causing global cataclysm. The Celestial Worthy of Heaven and Earth
and God Almighty fought over planetary authorities, leading to mutual
destruction. Their residual wills and Sefirot were sealed across the Earth.
The First Epoch followed: a period of unbridled supernatural lawlessness where
the 22 pathways first differentiated from the Creator's remains.

### Second Epoch — Dark Epoch

Rise of the Ancient Gods — monstrous, non-human entities with massive power
but no sanity. Ruled by beings like the Giant King Aurmir and the Elf King
Soniathrym. Humans existed as a subservient slave race.

### Third Epoch — Cataclysm Epoch

The Ancient Sun God awakened in the ruins of Chernobyl. He reclaimed
authorities from the Ancient Gods and led humanity in a successful revolt,
establishing himself as a "Half-Pillar." He was betrayed and killed by his
own Kings of Angels, leading to the emergence of several modern orthodox
deities. The Forsaken Land of the Gods was sealed from the sun during this
epoch.

### Fourth Epoch — Epoch of the Gods

Frequent divine descent into the physical world. Rise of powerful empires:
the Solomon Empire and the Tudor-Trunsoest United Empire. Massive divine wars
including the War of the Four Emperors and the Pale Disaster shaped current
geography and political boundaries.

### Fifth Epoch — Iron Age (Current Setting)

Defined by the Industrial Revolution, concealment of mysticism from the
general public, and dominance of the Seven Orthodox Churches. Steam power is
ascendant. The world is post-apocalyptic Earth, reshaped by millennia of
divine combat.

## Above the Sequence: Great Old Ones and Outer Deities

The pinnacle of existence. These beings have integrated multiple pathways and a
corresponding Sefirah, becoming representations of fundamental aspects of the
universe.

### The Three Pillars

| Pillar                    | Title                                        |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| God Almighty              | The Omnipotent and Omniscient; oversees the physical and astral worlds |
| Lord of Mysteries         | King of Space-Time and Beacon of Destiny; oversees the spirit world |
| Mother Goddess of Depravity | Origin of Evil and Sovereign of the Physical World |

### Outer Deities

Currently surrounding the solar system, waiting for Earth's protective barrier
to collapse:

- **Mother Tree of Desire** — corrupts the Indulgence Faction of the Rose
  School of Thought
- **Son of Chaos**
- **Circle of Inevitability** — central to the sequel's narrative

Even their names or symbols can cause madness in low-sequence Beyonders.

## The Sequel: Circle of Inevitability

Set six years after Book 1. Protagonist is Lumian Lee, caught in a nightmarish
time loop in the village of Cordu orchestrated by an Outer Deity.

### The Boon System

A significant alternative to potions. Boons are direct gifts of power from
Outer Deities (Circle of Inevitability, Mother Goddess of Depravity, etc.).
They allow rapid growth but carry extreme risk of total corruption and loss
of autonomy — the recipient becomes a literal "puppet" of the divine.

### Lumian Lee

A dual-pathway Beyonder: Red Priest pathway (potions) and Inevitability/Dancer
pathway (boons). Darker, more pragmatic tone than Klein's story. Navigates
the "madness" phase of the world as the protective barrier thins. Eventually
reaches dual-pathway Sequence 1, intended as a weapon or vessel in the
upcoming cosmic war.
