## Three Laws of Beyonder Characteristics

1. **Indestructibility** — The total volume of characteristics in the cosmos is
   constant. They cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred. When a
   Beyonder dies their characteristics condense into a recoverable item.

2. **Conservation (Similar-Sequence)** — Zero-sum hierarchy within each of the
   22 pathways. Advancing to Sequence 0 requires absorbing all three Sequence 1
   characteristics for that pathway, leaving no Sequence 1 Angels while a god
   occupies the throne.

3. **Convergence (Aggregation)** — Characteristics of the same or neighboring
   pathways are unconsciously drawn together. This functions as "fate,"
   explaining why Beyonders frequently encounter artifacts and enemies relevant
   to their next advancement.

## The Potion System and the Acting Method

- Each pathway has 10 sequences: Sequence 9 (weakest) to Sequence 0 (True God).
- Drinking a potion without proper preparation causes loss of control —
  the Beyonder collapses under the Original Creator's residual mental imprints.
- The **Acting Method** requires behaving according to the sequence name's
  symbolic meaning (e.g., a "Seer" performs divinations, a "Clown" maintains
  a humorous facade). The practitioner must "remember they are only acting."

## Setting Context (Fifth Epoch — Iron Age)

- Industrial revolution / Victorian steampunk era
- Mysticism hidden from the general public
- Four major nations: Loen Kingdom (Britain), Intis Republic (France),
  Feysac Empire (Russia), Feynapotter Kingdom
- Seven Orthodox Churches control official Beyonder organizations
- Secret organizations operate in the shadows (Twilight Hermit Order,
  Aurora Order, Rose School of Thought, Psychology Alchemists, etc.)

## Above the Sequence

- Great Old Ones / Outer Deities have integrated multiple pathways + a Sefirah
- Three Pillars: God Almighty, Lord of Mysteries, Mother Goddess of Depravity
- Outer Deities (Mother Tree of Desire, Son of Chaos, Circle of Inevitability)
  surround Earth waiting for the protective barrier to collapse
